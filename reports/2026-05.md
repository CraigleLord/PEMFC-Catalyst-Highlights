## 1. Acidic oxygen reduction by single-atom Fe catalysts on curved supports

**Authors:** Yasong Zhao, Jiawei Wan, Chongyi Ling, Dan Wang et al.
**Journal:** *Nature* 644, 668–675 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1038/s41586-025-09364-6](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-025-09364-6)

![Key figure](https://scienmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Single-Atom-Fe-Boosts-Acidic-Oxygen-Reduction-1755145273.jpg)

- Fe single atoms are positioned on the **concave inner surface of nanoprotrusions** on 2D carbon layers, rather than on the flat basal plane.
- The curved geometry geometrically weakens binding of oxygenated intermediates (*OH, *OOH), moving the Fe site closer to the ORR thermodynamic optimum.
- Curved confinement simultaneously suppresses Fenton-type hydroxyl radical generation — the primary driver of carbon corrosion and Fe demetallation in acid.
- In H₂–air PEMFC at atmospheric pressure, the catalyst reached a **record PGM-free power density of 0.75 W cm⁻²**, with **86% activity retained after 300 h** of continuous operation.
- This is the first demonstration that support curvature at the atomic scale can simultaneously boost activity and durability for acidic ORR — opening a new geometric-engineering design axis independent of chemical composition.

## 2. Regulating in situ gaseous deposition to construct highly durable Fe–N–C oxygen-reduction fuel cell catalysts

**Authors:** Zeng Y., Qi M., Liang J. et al.
**Journal:** *Nature Catalysis* 9, 196–210 (2026) · [DOI: 10.1038/s41929-026-01482-2](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41929-026-01482-2)

- An **in situ chemical vapor deposition (CVD)** strategy is introduced during Fe–N–C catalyst pyrolysis, depositing carbon species that partially fill surface micropores while Fe–Nx sites form.
- Reduced micropore density physically restricts Fe leaching pathways and reduces the rate of carbon corrosion — the two dominant degradation modes of Fe–N–C materials in acid.
- The resulting catalyst demonstrates both high initial ORR activity and **dramatically improved durability** in PEMFC operation versus conventional Fe–N–C without in situ CVD.
- Crucially, the synthesis handle (gas-phase deposition atmosphere during pyrolysis) is simple to implement on existing reactor setups, making this approach scalable.
- Establishes a new principle: controlling the **porosity-activity balance** through gaseous deposition to decouple the traditional activity–stability trade-off in non-precious-metal catalysts.

## 3. Antisite defect unleashes catalytic potential in high-entropy intermetallics for oxygen reduction reaction

**Authors:** Tao Chen, Xinkai Zhang, Hangchao Wang et al.
**Journal:** *Nature Communications* 16, 3308 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1038/s41467-025-58679-5](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-58679-5)

![Key figure — electrochemical performance](https://cdn.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/blobs/0551/11977229/73a70ff3d9c3/41467_2025_58679_Fig3_HTML.jpg)

- A **PtZnFeCoNiCr high-entropy intermetallic** is synthesized with controlled Pt antisite point defects, exploiting the sluggish diffusion in high-entropy systems to preserve long-range order while placing Pt in off-stoichiometric positions.
- Pt antisite defects impose **compressive strain** on neighboring Pt sites and down-shift the d-band center, optimizing OH* binding energy; adjacent non-noble metal sites are simultaneously activated as secondary catalytic centers.
- Half-cell mass activity reached **4.12 A mg⁻¹_Pt** — 33× commercial Pt/C — far exceeding the 2025 US DOE target.
- PEMFC testing yielded a **peak power density of 1.9 W cm⁻²** and mass activity of 3.0 A mg⁻¹_Pt at 0.9 V.
- Demonstrates defect engineering in high-entropy intermetallics as a strategy to exploit multiple active metal centers simultaneously, beyond what any binary or ternary alloy can offer.

## 4. Sandwiching intermetallic Pt₃Fe and ionomer with porous N-doped carbon layers for oxygen reduction reaction

**Authors:** Xiaoqing Cao, Hongyu Guo, Ying Han et al.
**Journal:** *Nature Communications* 16, 2851 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1038/s41467-025-58116-7](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-58116-7)

![Key figure — ORR activity](https://cdn.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/blobs/d38e/11931003/25ee3f3cad66/41467_2025_58116_Fig3_HTML.jpg)

- **Pt₃Fe intermetallic nanoparticles** are encapsulated by an atomically thin, porous N-doped carbon shell that acts as a selective molecular sieve.
- The carbon shell physically blocks Nafion ionomer sulfonate groups from adsorbing onto Pt surface sites — one of the most significant but frequently overlooked mass-transport losses in real MEAs.
- N-coordination of the shell also **anchors surface Fe atoms**, suppressing transition-metal leaching into the membrane and the associated Fenton chemistry.
- The catalyst drove a H₂–air PEMFC to **1.0 W cm⁻² peak power density** with only **19.1% activity degradation after 100 h** of operation.
- Addresses ionomer poisoning as a discrete, engineerable degradation pathway rather than an unavoidable property of Nafion-coated MEAs.

## 5. Dynamic modulation of electron redistribution at the Ni(OH)₂/Pt interface boosts acidic oxygen reduction

**Authors:** Meihuan Liu, Hui Su, Xuanzhi Liu et al.
**Journal:** *Nature Communications* 16, 2826 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1038/s41467-025-58193-8](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-58193-8)

![Key figure — ORR activity in 0.1 M HClO₄](https://cdn.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/blobs/32a4/11929921/50b2e4283b6a/41467_2025_58193_Fig3_HTML.jpg)

- A **mesoporous amorphous-crystalline Ni(OH)₂/Pt heterojunction** (ac-Ni(OH)₂@m-Pt) is synthesized where the amorphous Ni(OH)₂ shell wraps crystalline Pt cores with intimate contact.
- In situ XAS and synchrotron IR spectroscopy reveal that the amorphous/crystalline interface **dynamically redistributes electrons** between Ni and Pt under applied potential, a phenomenon absent in all-crystalline or all-amorphous counterparts.
- This dynamic electron flow accelerates O₂ activation and protonation of *OOH intermediates, lowering the kinetic barriers along the dominant ORR pathway.
- Half-cell mass activity of **0.95 A mg⁻¹_Pt** in 0.1 M HClO₄, maintaining **89.8% retention after 15,000 ADT cycles**.
- Opens an under-explored design space: **amorphous-crystalline heterointerfaces** as a source of potential-responsive electronic tunability in Pt-based catalysts.

## 6. Interfacial metal–coordinated bifunctional PtCo for practical fuel cells

**Authors:** Zhongliang Huang, Qi Xiao, Tianyi Ding et al.
**Journal:** *Science Advances* 11, eadt4914 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1126/sciadv.adt4914](https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.adt4914)

- A PtCo intermetallic catalyst is deposited on a **Sn–N–C support** that forms direct Pt–Sn–N coordination bonds at the metal-support interface, providing a bifunctional active site.
- The Sn-mediated metal–support interaction boosts ORR intrinsic activity through ligand effects while simultaneously providing surface sites that **bind and oxidize CO**, enabling tolerance to contaminated hydrogen feeds.
- H₂–O₂ MEA achieved a **mass activity of 0.94 A mg⁻¹_Pt at 0.9 V** (>2× the 2025 DOE target) and peak power of 1.33 W cm⁻².
- After 30,000 voltage-cycling AST, the catalyst retained **97.9% of initial mass activity** — surpassing the DOE durability target, which almost no Pt-alloy catalyst has achieved.
- Ran stably for >710 h on 100 ppm CO-contaminated H₂ with 2.11 W cm⁻² peak power — directly relevant to practical hydrogen supply chains where fuel purity varies.

## 7. Janus effect of FeCo dual-atom catalyst with Co as active center in acidic oxygen reduction

**Authors:** (Lead: CAS/Tsinghua group)
**Journal:** *Nature Communications* 16 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1038/s41467-025-62728-4](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-62728-4)

- Strongly bonded **Fe–Co diatomic pairs** are embedded in N-doped carbon via molecular chelation and ionic coupling; the two metal atoms have distinct and complementary roles — a "Janus effect."
- In situ spectroscopy + DFT reveal **Co acts as the primary 4e⁻ ORR center** while **Fe preferentially adsorbs and desorbs *OH**, reducing the energy barrier for the rate-limiting step without hosting the full ORR cycle itself.
- This site-specific division of labor suppresses H₂O₂ generation (2e⁻ pathway) and the Fenton reaction simultaneously — addressing both the activity and stability shortcomings of Fe-only N–C catalysts.
- The catalyst achieved a half-wave potential of **0.852 V** and a PEMFC power density of **1.14 W cm⁻²** (H₂–O₂, 2.0 bar), retaining 81% of peak power after 10,000 cycles.
- Provides mechanistic clarity for rational dual-atom catalyst design: rather than treating diatomic pairs as a symmetric active site, the field should assign and engineer distinct roles to each atom.

## 8. Transforming Single-Atom to Dual-Atom Sites in Fe–N–C Catalysts: A Universal Durability Strategy

**Authors:** Ruguang Wang et al.
**Journal:** *Angewandte Chemie International Edition* 64, e202510671 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1002/anie.202510671](https://doi.org/10.1002/anie.202510671)

- A **post-synthesis H₂/Ar annealing** treatment selectively breaks C–N bonds near FeN₄ single-atom sites and inserts additional Fe and N, converting isolated FeN₄ moieties into **Fe₂N₆ dual-atom sites**.
- Fe₂N₆ dual-atom sites are geometrically constrained to produce **<1% H₂O₂ selectivity** and show an extremely low Fe demetallation ratio of only 0.61 at% after 80,000 potential cycles in 0.1 M HClO₄.
- The transformed PEMFC ran stably for **300 h with only 7% current density loss**, versus 84% decline within 100 h for pristine FeN₄-based MEAs — a **12-fold durability improvement**.
- The transformation works across a wide range of carbon substrates (graphene, CNT, hard carbon), making it directly applicable to existing Fe–N–C libraries without resynthesis.
- Positions the Fe₂N₆ coordination environment as a durability-optimized target for the Fe–N–C field, analogous to how intermetallic ordering became the target for Pt-alloy catalysts.

## 9. Dual-Atom Catalysts for ORR: Unraveling Atomic Structures under Reaction Conditions

**Authors:** Courtney Brea, Guoxiang Hu
**Journal:** *Journal of the American Chemical Society* 147, 19210–19216 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1021/jacs.5c04776](https://doi.org/10.1021/jacs.5c04776)

- A computational screening of **186 FeM–N–C dual-atom catalyst variants** (M = Mn, Co, Ni, Cu, Zn, Pt) uses ab initio thermodynamic phase diagrams to map which structural configurations dominate under actual electrochemical potentials.
- The critical finding: **OH-ligated structures become the predominant active species at higher applied potentials**, meaning static bare-metal site models systematically misidentify the true catalytic center.
- This mismatch explains why DFT-predicted activities based on static structures often contradict experimental measurements — the field has been modeling the wrong structure.
- Among all 186 candidates, **FeCo–N–C** showed the highest computed ORR limiting potential; 29 configurations outperformed Pt benchmarks.
- Establishes **potential-dependent phase diagrams** as a predictive descriptor for dual-atom ORR catalyst screening, and calls for operando rather than ex situ structural characterization as the field standard.

## 10. Establishing the stability number descriptor for Fe–N–C fuel cell electrocatalysts

**Authors:** Yu-Ping Ku, Kavita Kumar, Antoine Bonnefont et al.
**Journal:** *Chemical Science* 16 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1039/D5SC00547G](https://doi.org/10.1039/D5SC00547G)

- The **"stability number" (S-number)** — ratio of electrons exchanged during ORR to dissolved Fe atoms — is introduced as a quantitative, operationally defined descriptor for Fe–N–C catalyst stability.
- In alkaline media the S-number is constant across current density and temperature, making it a robust intrinsic descriptor; in acidic media it varies with current density due to **local pH gradients** inside the catalyst layer.
- Kinetic modeling revealed that higher ORR currents raise local pH near catalyst particles, promoting iron hydroxide redeposition — a previously unrecognized **self-stabilization mechanism** in acid.
- The S-number can be measured on any rotating-disk electrode or MEA setup with online ICP-MS, making it immediately deployable as a standardized durability benchmark.
- Provides the Fe–N–C field with a unified, scalable diagnostic equivalent to the specific activity and mass activity metrics used to benchmark Pt-based catalysts.

## 11. Emerging strategies for durable Pt catalysts in PEMFCs

**Authors:** Yuliang Chen, Linghang Meng, Haobo Sun et al.
**Journal:** *Chemical Science* 17, issue 6 (2026) · [DOI: 10.1039/D5SC08221H](https://doi.org/10.1039/D5SC08221H)

![Overview figure](https://pubs.rsc.org/en/image/article/2026/SC/d5sc08221h/d5sc08221h-f1_hi-res.gif)

- A comprehensive perspective mapping **four principal Pt degradation pathways** — metal dissolution, Ostwald ripening/agglomeration, carbon support corrosion, and surface poisoning — and evaluating emerging countermeasures for each.
- **Ordered intermetallics and high-entropy alloys** are identified as the most effective route against dissolution, reducing Pt and TM dissolution rates by up to two orders of magnitude vs. disordered alloys.
- Surface confinement with porous carbon shells simultaneously addresses Ostwald ripening and support corrosion, but shell synthesis must be carefully tuned to avoid blocking oxygen transport.
- **Heteroatom-doped carbon supports** (N, B, S) provide anchor points that strongly suppress Pt nanoparticle migration; metal-oxide/carbon composites offer additional corrosion resistance.
- Machine learning-guided catalyst screening and advanced operando characterization are identified as the key future enablers for reaching the **30,000-hour heavy-duty vehicle lifetime** target.

## 12. Highly loaded fine PtCo intermetallic compounds on 3D N-doped porous graphene for enhanced and sustained PEMFC efficiency

**Authors:** Zhihao Wang, Huihui Jin, Yizhou Hao et al.
**Journal:** *Journal of Materials Chemistry A* 14, issue 17 (2026) · [DOI: 10.1039/D5TA05492C](https://doi.org/10.1039/D5TA05492C)

- **Plasma-enhanced CVD** is used to grow highly graphitized N-doped porous graphene as a support, achieving simultaneously high conductivity, strong corrosion resistance, and N-doping anchor sites — properties that are usually in tension with conventional carbon blacks.
- The N-doping sites strongly anchor PtCo nanoparticles (~4.4 nm) even at **45.9 wt% Pt loading**, maintaining uniform dispersion that is impossible on un-doped graphene at comparable loading.
- L1₀-ordered intermetallic PtCo on this support yielded PEMFC **peak power density of 964 mW cm⁻²** with voltage losses of only 17–19 mV at critical current densities.
- The catalyst surpassed both the 2025 US DOE activity **and** stability targets — one of few reports to meet both simultaneously at high Pt loading.
- Demonstrates that support synthesis methodology (plasma CVD graphitization) directly dictates whether high Pt loading translates to durability, not just initial activity.

## 13. From Seeds to Cell: PtNiIr ORR Nanocatalysts via Seed-Mediated Synthesis

**Authors:** Lujin Pan, Thomas Merzdorf, Carlos A. Campos-Roldán et al.
**Journal:** *Advanced Science* 12, 2505958 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1002/advs.202505958](https://doi.org/10.1002/advs.202505958)

- **Ir nanoparticle seeds** template PtNi growth, yielding ternary PtNiIr particles (4–5 nm) substantially smaller and more compositionally uniform than one-pot synthesis products (6.6 nm).
- Particles deposited **inside porous carbon supports** (rather than on the exterior) retained **70–75% ECSA** after standardized durability protocols, versus ~50% for exterior-deposited particles — pore walls physically impede agglomeration.
- Online ICP-MS during AST recorded dramatically lower Ni and Ir dissolution from seed-mediated samples, directly linking the synthesis route to reduced membrane contamination.
- The Ir minority component (< 10 at%) appears to serve a dual role: nucleation template during synthesis and surface stabilizer (Ir is the most corrosion-resistant platinum-group metal) during operation.
- The seed-mediated approach is extendable to other ternary Pt–TM–Ir systems and provides a practical handle for automotive-scale catalyst manufacture where tight particle-size control is essential.

## 14. Toward Hydrogen Mobility: Challenges and Strategies in Electrocatalyst Durability for Long-Term PEMFC Operation

**Authors:** Subin Park, Eungjun Lee, Yoonsu Park, Myeong-Geun Kim, Sung Jong Yoo (KIST)
**Journal:** *JACS Au* 5, issue 4 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1021/jacsau.5c00173](https://doi.org/10.1021/jacsau.5c00173)

![Overview schematic](https://cdn.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/blobs/9419/12042041/e6326c91e452/au5c00173_0001.jpg)

![Durability strategies figure](https://cdn.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/blobs/9419/12042041/db916101a528/au5c00173_0005.jpg)

- A KIST perspective **benchmarking current catalyst technologies** against the demanding 30,000-hour heavy-duty vehicle lifetime target — the most stringent durability standard in the field.
- Three main durability-enhancement classes are rigorously assessed: **Pt intermetallics** (ordered alloys reduce TM dissolution 10–100×), **carbon-shell encapsulation** (prevents Ostwald ripening), and **Pt/M-N-C hybrids** (M–N–C acts as ORR co-catalyst and Pt anchor simultaneously).
- Alternative supports (graphitized carbon, metal oxides, carbides) are evaluated against standard Vulcan XC-72 on both corrosion resistance and electronic conductivity — graphitized carbon emerges as the most balanced option for near-term adoption.
- The article concludes that **no single-strategy approach** reaches 30,000 h; combining intermetallic ordering, shell protection, and corrosion-resistant support engineering is required.
- Provides an accessible roadmap for the field with quantitative degradation rates and protocol-by-protocol comparisons of the current state-of-the-art.

## 15. Platinum-based nanoalloys for ORR: exposing the true active phase via in situ/operando techniques

**Authors:** Carlos A. Campos-Roldán, Raphaël Chattot, Pierre-Yves Blanchard et al.
**Journal:** *Physical Chemistry Chemical Physics* 27, 6400–6407 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1039/D4CP03665D](https://doi.org/10.1039/D4CP03665D)

- In situ/operando techniques — **synchrotron XRD, SAXS, and electrochemical quartz crystal microbalance** — are applied simultaneously to track structural and compositional evolution of PtNi, PtCo, and PtFe nanoalloys during electrochemical activation and cycling.
- The catalytically active phase differs **substantially** from the as-synthesized structure: non-Pt leaching and Pt surface reconstruction produce a Pt-rich skin whose strain state and composition depend strongly on activation protocol.
- Operando data show that conventional ex situ characterization **systematically misidentifies the active structure**, explaining historical inconsistencies between DFT-predicted and experimentally measured activities.
- Particle size evolution tracked by SAXS reveals that agglomeration and dissolution onset at different potentials for PtNi vs. PtCo vs. PtFe — the alloy composition controls which degradation mode dominates first.
- Provides a clear **operando-first characterization protocol** and argues for abandoning purely static structural descriptors in ORR catalyst design: the field should characterize the catalyst under reaction conditions, not before.
