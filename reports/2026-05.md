## 1. Regulating orbital interaction to construct quasi-covalent bond networks in Pt intermetallic alloys for high-performance fuel cells

**Authors:** Xuan Liu, Yuhan Wang, Hu He et al.
**Journal:** *Nature Communications* 16, 4895 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1038/s41467-025-60171-z](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-60171-z)

- L1₀-PtCoCr/C achieves a mass activity of **1.27 A mg⁻¹_Pt** at 0.9 V — 5.3× commercial Pt/C and 1.3× binary L1₀-PtCo/C — with peak PEMFC power density of **1.40 W cm⁻²** at an ultra-low loading of 0.075 mg_Pt cm⁻².
- Rated power reaches **16.5 W mg⁻¹_Pt** under light-duty vehicle protocol — among the highest Pt mass power densities reported.
- Mass activity loss of only **~3%** after 30,000 AST cycles; binary L1₀-PtCo/C loses 52% under identical conditions.
- The ternary Cr additive forms a quasi-covalent bond network that suppresses Co anodic dissolution and inhibits Pt oxidation through an "electron buffering" effect, raising the interlayer diffusion energy barrier by **68%**.
- Demonstrates that orbital-interaction engineering via a third element is a viable route to simultaneously surpass the activity and durability of binary Pt-alloy intermetallics.

## 2. Antisite defect unleashes catalytic potential in high-entropy intermetallics for oxygen reduction reaction

**Authors:** Tao Chen, Xinkai Zhang, Hangchao Wang et al.
**Journal:** *Nature Communications* 16, 3308 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1038/s41467-025-58679-5](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-58679-5)

![Key figure](https://cdn.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/blobs/0551/11977229/73a70ff3d9c3/41467_2025_58679_Fig3_HTML.jpg)

- PtZnFeCoNiCr high-entropy intermetallic (HEI) with controlled Pt antisite defects achieves a half-cell mass activity of **4.12 A mg⁻¹_Pt** — **33×** commercial Pt/C — and half-wave potential of **0.948 V** vs. RHE.
- PEMFC peak power density of **1.9 W cm⁻²** (H₂–O₂) at 0.05 mg_Pt cm⁻²; MEA mass activity **3.0 A mg⁻¹_Pt** at 0.9 V — exceeds the 2025 US DOE target by 7×.
- H₂–air power density 0.652 W cm⁻²; chronoamperometric retention **90.1%** after 100,000 s; E₁/₂ decay only **7 mV** after 20,000 AST cycles.
- Sluggish diffusion in the HEI system enables controllable Pt antisite formation by tuning heat-treatment temperature; Pt antisites impose compressive strain and activate neighboring non-noble metal sites as secondary ORR centers.
- Establishes defect engineering in HEIs as a means of exploiting multiple active metal centers simultaneously, beyond what binary or ternary alloys can provide.

## 3. Sub-angstrom strain in high-entropy intermetallic boosts the oxygen reduction reaction in fuel cell cathodes

**Authors:** Xueru Zhao, Hao Cheng, Lijun Wu et al.
**Journal:** *Nature Communications* 16, 7547 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1038/s41467-025-62725-7](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-62725-7)

- N-doped L1₀-ordered PtCoNiFeCu/Ketjenblack (N-HEI/KB): half-wave potential **925 mV**, mass activity **1.45 A mg⁻¹** (6.0× Pt/C), specific activity **2.37 mA cm⁻²** (8.2× Pt/C).
- E₁/₂ decay only **5 mV** after 30,000 ADT cycles; 17.9% MA loss vs. 37.5% for Pt/C.
- MEA performance: **1,388 mA cm⁻²** at 0.7 V after **90,000 cycles** under heavy-duty vehicle (HDV) conditions — exceeds the DOE Million Mile Fuel Cell Truck target of 1,300 mA cm⁻².
- Metal–N bonds at the N-dopant sites create a "pinning effect" that stabilizes sub-angstrom compressive strain in the HEI lattice, preventing strain relaxation during cycling.
- Characterised at NSLS-II synchrotron; provides direct atomic-level evidence linking lattice strain magnitude to ORR activity in HEI systems.

## 4. Dynamic modulation of electron redistribution at the heterogeneous Ni(OH)₂/Pt interface boosts acidic oxygen reduction

**Authors:** Meihuan Liu, Hui Su, Xuanzhi Liu et al.
**Journal:** *Nature Communications* 16, 2826 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1038/s41467-025-58193-8](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-58193-8)

![Key figure](https://cdn.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/blobs/32a4/11929921/50b2e4283b6a/41467_2025_58193_Fig3_HTML.jpg)

- Amorphous-crystalline Ni(OH)₂@mesoporous-Pt (ac-Ni(OH)₂@m-Pt): half-wave potential **0.912 V** (+57 mV vs. commercial Pt/C), mass activity **0.95 A mg⁻¹_Pt** (7.9× Pt/C).
- Mass activity retention **89.8%** after 15,000 AST cycles; E₁/₂ attenuation only 7 mV; ECSA retention ~92%; 4-electron selectivity confirmed (n = 3.98, H₂O₂ yield < 4%).
- PEMFC maximum power density: **1.07 W cm⁻²**.
- In situ XAS and synchrotron IR reveal the amorphous–crystalline interface **dynamically redistributes electrons** under applied potential, accelerating *O intermediate protonation and lowering the rate-limiting kinetic barrier.
- Opens an under-explored design space: amorphous–crystalline heterointerfaces as a source of potential-responsive electronic tunability in Pt catalysts.

## 5. Boosting oxygen reduction performance in Pd-based metallenes by co-confining interstitial H and p-block single atoms

**Authors:** Yu Qiu, Mingzi Sun, Jiandong Wu et al.
**Journal:** *Nature Communications* 16, 5262 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1038/s41467-025-60400-5](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-60400-5)

- PdPbHx metallene mass activity: **1.36 A mg⁻¹** at 0.95 V — **46.9×** commercial Pt/C; specific activity 1.59 mA cm⁻²; half-wave potential 0.958 V in acidic half-cell.
- After 50,000 cycles: **95%** MA retention and 96% SA retention; 90.6% current retention after 30,000 s — exceptional stability for a non-Pt catalyst.
- Interstitial H atoms facilitate direct O₂ dissociation (dissociative pathway), bypassing the conventional scaling-relation constraint; single Pb atoms enhance O₂ adsorption.
- The 2D metallene architecture maximises active-site exposure; interstitial H confinement is confirmed by neutron diffraction and DFT.
- Provides a new materials class — group-10 metallenes with co-confined interstitials — for high-activity, low-PGM ORR catalysts.

## 6. Janus effect of FeCo dual-atom catalyst with Co as active center in acidic oxygen reduction

**Authors:** (Corresponding authors: CAS/Tsinghua group)
**Journal:** *Nature Communications* 16 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1038/s41467-025-62728-4](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-62728-4)

- Strongly bonded Fe–Co diatomic pairs embedded in N-doped carbon display a "Janus effect": **Co is the primary 4e⁻ ORR center** while **Fe selectively manages *OH adsorption/desorption**, lowering the rate-limiting barrier without hosting the full catalytic cycle.
- Asymmetric electronic structure and bidirectional Fe↔Co charge transfer suppress H₂O₂ generation (2e⁻ pathway) and Fenton chemistry simultaneously — addressing both activity and stability shortcomings of Fe-only N–C catalysts.
- Half-wave potential **0.852 V** in 0.1 M HClO₄ (acidic, PEMFC-relevant); PEMFC power density **1.14 W cm⁻²** (H₂–O₂, 2.0 bar); 81% power retention after 10,000 cycles.
- In situ spectroscopy + DFT confirm the site-specific mechanistic assignment — a rare case where each atom in a diatomic pair has been given a distinct, experimentally validated catalytic role.
- Provides a rational design principle for dual-atom catalysts: engineer functional asymmetry between the two metal atoms rather than treating diatomic sites as symmetric.

## 7. Regulating in situ gaseous deposition to construct highly durable Fe–N–C oxygen-reduction fuel cell catalysts

**Authors:** Yachao Zeng, Manman Qi, Jiashun Liang et al.
**Journal:** *Nature Catalysis* 9, 196–210 (2026) · [DOI: 10.1038/s41929-026-01482-2](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41929-026-01482-2)

- In situ chemical vapour deposition (iCVD) during Fe–N–C pyrolysis simultaneously increases Fe–Nx active site density and reduces surface micropore density — physically restricting Fe leaching and mitigating carbon corrosion, the two dominant degradation modes.
- Half-wave potential **0.867 V** — **unchanged** after a 100,000-cycle rotating-disk electrode AST; MEA H₂–air performance **93 mA cm⁻²** at 0.8 V after standard 30,000-cycle AST.
- Extended 120,000-cycle AST: only **2.9%** current loss at 0.6 V and **14.2%** at 0.7 V — the most durable Fe–N–C MEA performance reported to date.
- The iCVD atmosphere (hydrocarbon gas mixture) is the single tunable handle that decouples active-site density from porosity — normally in direct conflict — without changing the bulk precursor chemistry.
- Establishes a scalable synthetic principle (gas-phase deposition during pyrolysis) that decouples the classical activity–stability trade-off in non-precious-metal PEMFC catalysts.

## 8. Transforming Single-Atom to Dual-Atom Sites in Fe–N–C Catalysts: A Universal Strategy for Enhancing Durability in PEMFCs

**Authors:** Y. Zhao, P. Yin, Y. Yang, R. Wang et al.
**Journal:** *Angewandte Chemie International Edition* 64, e202510671 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1002/anie.202510671](https://doi.org/10.1002/anie.202510671)

- Post-synthesis H₂/Ar annealing breaks C–N bonds near FeN₄ single-atom sites and inserts additional Fe and N, converting isolated FeN₄ moieties into **Fe₂N₆ dual-atom sites** across any carbon substrate.
- Fe₂N₆ produces **<1% H₂O₂** and exhibits an Fe demetallation ratio of only **0.61 at%** after 80,000 potential cycles in 0.1 M HClO₄.
- PEMFC durability: **300 h continuous operation with only 7% current density loss**; pristine FeN₄-based MEAs declined **84%** within 100 h — a 12-fold improvement.
- The transformation is universal across ZIF-8, polyacrylonitrile, and phenolic resin carbon precursors, making it directly applicable to any existing Fe–N–C catalyst library without resynthesis.
- Positions Fe₂N₆ coordination as the durability-optimised target for Fe–N–C design — analogous to how intermetallic ordering became the standard for Pt-alloy catalysts.

## 9. Creating Favorable Pt/Co Interfaces via a Two-Step Approach for Constructing Highly Durable PtCo Intermetallic Fuel Cell Catalysts

**Authors:** Jiashun Liang, Haoran Yu, Michael J. Zachman et al.
**Journal:** *Advanced Materials* 38, 2510847 (2026) · [DOI: 10.1002/adma.202510847](https://doi.org/10.1002/adma.202510847)

- A two-step amino-acid-assisted synthesis produces L1₂-Pt₃Co with increased ordering degree and ultrafine particles at processing temperatures **below 800 °C** — resolving the classical trade-off between crystalline ordering and particle sintering.
- Highest mass activity: **1.5 A mg⁻¹_Pt** for L1₂-Pt₃Co/NC-Cys.
- MEA testing under heavy-duty vehicle (HDV) conditions: **1.35 A cm⁻²** at 0.7 V; current density loss only **7%** after 150,000-cycle extended AST — a record for PtCo catalysts under HDV protocol.
- Amino acid ligands in step 2 link Pt nanoparticles to Co via coordination bonds, creating favorable Pt/Co interfaces that promote L1₂ ordering at lower annealing temperature.
- Directly addresses the gap between light-duty (30,000 cycle) and heavy-duty (150,000 cycle) durability standards with a single catalyst platform.

## 10. O₂-Accessible Fe–N₄ Active Site Density Boosts Efficient Oxygen Reduction to Fuel-Cell Level

**Authors:** Zhang et al.
**Journal:** *Advanced Materials* (2026) · [DOI: 10.1002/adma.202521600](https://doi.org/10.1002/adma.202521600)

- Yolk-shell Fe/NC (y-Fe/NC) architecture maximises O₂-accessible Fe–N₄ site density: E₁/₂ of **0.82 V** in 0.5 M H₂SO₄; limiting current density **7.66 mA cm⁻²** vs. 5.16 for solid and 5.67 for hollow Fe/NC.
- PEMFC peak power density: **1.03 W cm⁻²** (H₂–O₂) and **0.34 W cm⁻²** (H₂–air).
- Internal void space of the yolk-shell geometry enhances O₂ mass transport to buried Fe–N₄ sites while the outer shell maintains structural integrity under PEMFC operating conditions.
- Establishes **O₂ accessibility** — not raw site density — as the key descriptor for Fe–N–C PEMFC performance, shifting the design target from maximizing site count to maximizing site utilization.
- Provides a straightforward geometric design principle (yolk-shell) that can be applied to any Fe–N–C synthesis route.

## 11. High-Yield Synthesis of Fe-NC Electrocatalysts Using Mg²⁺ Templating and Schiff-Base Porous Organic Polymers

**Authors:** Petitdemange et al.
**Journal:** *Advanced Functional Materials* (2026) · [DOI: 10.1002/adfm.202518944](https://doi.org/10.1002/adfm.202518944)

- 2,4,6-triaminopyrimidine-based porous organic polymer + Mg²⁺ Lewis acid + low-temperature cation exchange yields atomically dispersed FeN_x with minimal Fe aggregation and a synthetic yield exceeding **45%** — the highest reported for Mg²⁺-templated Fe–N–C systems (vs. 6% for molecular analogues).
- Specific surface area **> 1,000 m² g⁻¹** with hierarchical micro/meso/macropores for efficient mass transport.
- Gas diffusion electrode current density: **1 A cm⁻²** at **0.687 ± 0.004 V**_RHE — competitive with state-of-the-art Fe–N–C benchmarks.
- Fe–N_x site utilization of **52%**, achieved by decoupling precursor organization from metal coordination during pyrolysis.
- High synthetic yield at scale addresses a critical barrier to commercial Fe–N–C production for PEMFC applications.

## 12. Interfacial metal-coordinated bifunctional PtCo for practical fuel cells

**Authors:** Zhongliang Huang, Qi Xiao, Tianyi Ding et al.
**Journal:** *Science Advances* 11, eadt4914 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1126/sciadv.adt4914](https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.adt4914)

- PtCo intermetallic on Sn–N–C support forms Pt–Sn–N coordination bonds at the metal-support interface, providing simultaneous ORR activity enhancement and CO tolerance.
- Mass activity **1.65 A mg⁻¹** (vs. 0.27 for Pt/C); PEMFC peak power density **2.48 W cm⁻²** (H₂–O₂) and **1.33 W cm⁻²** (H₂–air).
- CO tolerance: **2.11 W cm⁻²** peak power density in 100 ppm CO/H₂–O₂ — **highest reported** for CO-tolerant PEMFCs; stable for **> 710 hours** in 100 ppm CO/H₂–air at rated voltage.
- MA retention **97.9%** after 30,000 voltage cycles — surpassing the DOE 2025 durability target and among the best results for any Pt-alloy cathode.
- The Sn-mediated bifunctional interface is broadly transferable to other Pt–TM intermetallics, providing a design strategy for CO-tolerant PEMFC catalysts relevant to reformate hydrogen feeds.

## 13. Gas Dynamically Confined Synthesis of Platinum-Based Intermetallic Nanowires for Active and Ultrastable Oxygen Reduction Catalysis

**Authors:** Yaming Liu, Zhenle Hu, Xinrui Zhu et al.
**Journal:** *ACS Nano* (2025) · [DOI: 10.1021/acsnano.5c10292](https://doi.org/10.1021/acsnano.5c10292)

- Gas-phase H₂ dynamically confines atomic migration during high-temperature annealing, preserving 1D morphology and exposing abundant high-index {311}/{211}/{221} facets in Pt₃Fe (L1₂), Pt₃Co (L1₂), and PtNi (L1₀) nanowires.
- Ordered Pt₃Fe nanowires: mass activity **0.98 A mg⁻¹_Pt** at 0.9 V; retains **93.9%** MA after 30,000 cycles.
- PEMFC power density retention **86.4%** after **70,000 voltage cycles** — exceptional stability for a 1D nanostructure in a real MEA.
- High-index facets expose under-coordinated Pt with optimized oxygen binding energy; intermetallic ordering prevents Co/Fe dissolution that normally accelerates in strained 1D structures.
- Synthesis is solvent-free and scalable (gas-phase precursor), making it attractive for industrial catalyst production.

## 14. Dual-Atom Catalysts for the Oxygen Reduction Reaction: Unraveling Atomic Structures under Reaction Conditions

**Authors:** Courtney Brea, Guoxiang Hu
**Journal:** *Journal of the American Chemical Society* 147, 19210–19216 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1021/jacs.5c04776](https://doi.org/10.1021/jacs.5c04776)

- Ab initio thermodynamic screening of **186 heteronuclear FeM–N–C dual-atom catalyst** variants (M = Mn, Co, Ni, Cu, Zn, Pt) maps which structural configurations dominate under actual electrochemical potentials.
- **OH-ligated structures become the predominant active species at higher applied potentials** — static bare-metal DFT models systematically misidentify the true catalytic center, explaining historical discrepancies between predicted and measured activities.
- **29 out of 186 DAC configurations** theoretically outperform Pt benchmarks; FeCo–N–C shows the highest predicted ORR limiting potential.
- FeNi–N–C (0,2) achieves an optimal computed limiting potential of **0.93 V** vs. RHE.
- Establishes potential-dependent phase diagrams as a predictive descriptor for DAC screening, and calls for operando rather than ex situ structural characterization as the field standard.

## 15. Dense Platinum-Based Intermetallic Nanoparticles Confined into Hollow Mesoporous Carbon for Durable High-Power Heavy-Duty Fuel Cells

**Authors:** (Technical Institute of Physics and Chemistry, CAS)
**Journal:** *Journal of the American Chemical Society* 148, 9563–9571 (2026) · [DOI: 10.1021/jacs.5c19791](https://doi.org/10.1021/jacs.5c19791)

- Ligand-tuned co-reduction loads Pt-based intermetallic compound (IMC) nanoparticles at **> 50 wt% metal** and **~3 nm** particle size with high ordering degree into hollow mesoporous carbon (HMC).
- HMC architecture provides oxygen mass-transport-favorable pathways to the confined IMC active sites — addressing the critical diffusion limitation of high-loading electrodes.
- Catalyst designed specifically to meet heavy-duty vehicle (HDV) requirements: **> 30,000 h lifetime**, 90,000-cycle durability target.
- The dense IMC + HMC combination simultaneously addresses the activity–stability trade-off that causes conventional solid-solution Pt catalysts to fail under HDV protocol.
- Demonstrates that support geometry (hollow vs. solid mesoporous carbon) is a first-order design variable for high-Pt-loading electrodes at HDV power densities.

## 16. Fe–N–C Electrocatalyst with d-π Interaction Induced by Submicropore Vacancies for Durable ORR in PEMFCs

**Authors:** Jiawang Li, Changqing Lin, Zhenyu Chen et al.
**Journal:** *ACS Catalysis* 15, 20512–20530 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.5c06689](https://doi.org/10.1021/acscatal.5c06689)

- MgCl₂·6H₂O-assisted pyrolysis generates submicropore vacancies (< 1.0 nm) that break FeN₄ molecular orbital symmetry, creating a **d-π interaction** between Fe 3d orbitals and the N-dopant π system.
- Half-wave potential **0.84 V** in 0.5 M H₂SO₄; E₁/₂ degradation only **7.0 mV** after 10,000 cycles; PEMFC maximum power output **822 mW cm⁻²**.
- d-π interaction stabilizes the Fe center, reducing demetallation and H₂O₂ generation under acidic PEMFC conditions — directly addressing the two dominant Fe–N–C failure modes.
- Degradation rate of **0.665 mA cm⁻²h⁻¹** — competitive with state-of-the-art Fe–N–C MEA performance.
- Identifies submicropore vacancy engineering (< 1 nm scale) as a distinct, controllable handle for improving Fe–N–C durability, separate from macro-/mesopore design.

## 17. Unravelling the Impact of the Ionomer on the Degradation Mechanisms in Carbon-Supported Platinum Electrocatalysts: On the Path Toward Durable PEMFCs

**Authors:** (European/academic consortium)
**Journal:** *ACS Catalysis* 15, 8611–8623 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.5c01466](https://doi.org/10.1021/acscatal.5c01466)

- Systematic study of ionomer effects on Pt/C degradation across three carbon supports (nonporous Vulcan, microporous Ketjenblack, mesoporous hollow graphitic spheres) during 80 °C voltage-cycling AST.
- Nonporous Vulcan: ionomer incorporation **enhances ECSA loss** — sulfonate groups adsorb directly on exposed Pt, promoting Ostwald ripening and particle coarsening.
- Microporous/mesoporous supports: ionomer **protects intra-pore Pt** while particles outside pores dissolve preferentially — particle location determines degradation mechanism, not just kinetics.
- First quantitative demonstration that ionomer type and carbon porosity interact **synergistically** in controlling Pt dissolution; the interaction cannot be captured by studying either variable alone.
- Directly actionable for MEA catalyst-layer design: porous carbons mitigate ionomer-induced degradation; sulfonate group management is critical for nonporous carbon supports.

## 18. Electrostatic Repulsion Activates Durable Pt Catalysts for HT-PEMFCs

**Authors:** H. Cheng et al.
**Journal:** *Advanced Energy Materials* (2026) · [DOI: 10.1002/aenm.70724](https://doi.org/10.1002/aenm.70724)

- Oxidized sulfur (SO_x) groups incorporated into the carbon support create a localized negative electrostatic environment around Pt active sites that **repels H₂PO₄⁻** — the primary poisoning species in high-temperature PEMFCs (120–200 °C, phosphoric acid electrolyte).
- DFT and spectroscopic analyses confirm both the strong Pt–support interaction and the spatial electrostatic shielding mechanism that excludes PA anions from Pt surface sites.
- Pt-SO_x/C outperforms conventional Pt/C under phosphoric acid-saturated HT-PEMFC operating conditions; PA poisoning normally causes **> 50%** performance loss in undoped catalysts.
- Addresses a failure mode largely orthogonal to low-temperature PEMFC degradation, but increasingly important as HT-PEMFCs are targeted for reformate/CO-tolerant applications.
- The SO_x functionalization strategy is compatible with standard Pt/C synthesis, requiring only a support pre-treatment step.

## 19. Breaking the symmetry of high-entropy alloy surfaces for compressively strain-tuned oxygen reduction reaction

**Authors:** (Chinese/international team)
**Journal:** *Nature Communications* 16, 10834 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1038/s41467-025-65856-z](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-65856-z)

- PtPdFeCoNi high-entropy alloy nanorings with terrace-type surface defects: mass activity **0.99 A mg⁻¹_PGM** and specific activity **1.32 mA cm⁻²** at 0.95 V vs. RHE.
- Breaking atomic symmetry at nanoring surfaces releases surface stress and induces controllable compressive strain, lowering the Pt d-band center and facilitating *OH desorption — the rate-limiting step in acidic ORR.
- Nanoring geometry provides abundant terrace-type surface sites; strain magnitude is tuneable by alloy composition without requiring high annealing temperatures.
- Stress-released compressive strain is a generalizable design principle for HEA nanocatalysts, complementary to the lattice-strain approach used in ordered intermetallics.
- Competitive performance among PGM-based HEA ORR catalysts at lower processing cost than thermally ordered HEIs.

## 20. Enhancing the ORR Durability of Single Atomic Fe–N₄ Active Sites with Implanted SiO₂ Nanoparticles as Radical and H₂O₂ Inhibitors

**Authors:** (Chinese academic team)
**Journal:** *Nature Communications* (2025) · [DOI: 10.1038/s41467-025-65194-0](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-65194-0)

- SiO₂ nanoparticles embedded in a Fe–N₄ SAC (synthesized from waste coffee grounds + industrial spent acid residue) act as **dual radical and H₂O₂ scavengers**, suppressing both primary Fe–N–C degradation pathways simultaneously.
- Fe–O–Si binding at the SiO₂–Fe–N₄ interface strengthens *OOH binding, enhancing 4-electron selectivity and reducing H₂O₂ production — a thermodynamic handle distinct from electronic doping.
- E₁/₂ loss only **5 mV** after 30,000 voltage cycles; SiO₂ NPs prevent Fe single-atom site aggregation and inhibit reactive oxygen species (ROS) attack on the carbon support.
- Demonstrates a sustainable synthesis approach using waste-derived precursors — the carbon host is synthesized entirely from industrial and food waste streams.
- Fe–O–Si interface is tunable: SiO₂ content adjusts the balance between H₂O₂ scavenging and 4-electron selectivity, providing a compositional design handle for site-specific durability engineering.

## 21. Pt₂Gd Alloy Nanoparticles from Organometallic Complexes and Hollow Mesoporous Carbon Spheres: Enhanced ORR Activity and Durability

**Authors:** (Japanese group)
**Journal:** *Journal of the American Chemical Society* 147, 1262–1270 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1021/jacs.4c15181](https://doi.org/10.1021/jacs.4c15181)

- Pt₂Gd alloy nanoparticles (5.9 nm avg.) in hollow mesoporous carbon spheres (HMCS) synthesized via thermal reduction of organometallic Pt + Gd complexes — ligand-free and contaminant-free.
- Mass activity **2.4× higher** than Pt/HMCS; remarkable durability after **100,000 ADT cycles** outperforming both Pt/HMCS and commercial Pt/C by large margin.
- Fully characterised by TEM, HAADF-STEM-EDS, XRD, XAFS, and XPS confirming Pt₂Gd stoichiometry and complete alloy formation.
- Gd as a rare-earth additive provides strong electronic modification of Pt 5d states; the stronger Pt–Gd bond vs. Pt–Co/Ni suppresses Gd dissolution and maintains the alloy structure under potential cycling.
- Opens an underexplored rare-earth direction for Pt-alloy ORR catalysts — most PEMFC Pt-alloy research focuses exclusively on 3d transition metals (Co, Ni, Fe, Cu).

## 22. Atomic-Level Insights Into the Durability of Ordered PtNi Intermetallic Electrocatalysts for ORR in PEMFCs

**Authors:** Woo et al.
**Journal:** *Small* (2026) · [DOI: 10.1002/smll.202512426](https://doi.org/10.1002/smll.202512426)

- L1₀-PtNi-Ni/ZrO₂ with reverse metal–support interaction (RMSI): MEA mass activity **0.76 A mg⁻¹_Pt** at 0.9 V (iR-free).
- Exceptional long-term durability: E₁/₂ loss only **~11 mV** after **400,000 potential cycles** — an order of magnitude more cycles than most reported durability tests; L1₀-PtNi-C loses 12 mV after only 100,000 cycles.
- DFT-based design reveals that the Ni/ZrO₂ substrate induces RMSI that stabilizes the L1₀ phase by preventing Ni segregation to the surface under high oxygen coverage.
- Core metal dissolution controlled by RMSI-driven segregation suppression — addresses the failure mode where intermetallic ordering alone is insufficient to prevent TM surface enrichment.
- Demonstrates that metal-oxide supports (ZrO₂) enabling RMSI are a viable path to ultra-long-cycle durability targets relevant to heavy-duty vehicle operation.
