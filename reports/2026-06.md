## 1. Carbon-Encapsulated PtCo Intermetallic/Co–N–C Hybrid Catalyst for Ultralow-Pt-Loading Fuel-Cell Catalysis

**Authors:** Yu et al.
**Journal:** *Advanced Energy Materials* (2026) · [DOI: 10.1002/aenm.202506060](https://doi.org/10.1002/aenm.202506060)

- PtCo intermetallic nanoparticles are encapsulated within a nitrogen-doped carbon (NC) shell and co-supported on a Co–N–C substrate, combining alloy ordering with PGM-free synergy at an ultralow cathode Pt loading of only **0.04 mg cm⁻²**.
- The hybrid catalyst achieves a mass activity of **1.56 A mg⁻¹Pt at 0.9 V (iR-free)** in rotating-disk electrode tests, among the highest reported for carbon-encapsulated intermetallics.
- Single-cell PEMFC accelerated stress testing (AST) of **60,000 potential cycles** results in only **4 mV voltage decay at 0.8 A cm⁻²**, demonstrating exceptional structural and compositional durability.
- The NC encapsulation layer suppresses Co leaching and Pt sintering during electrochemical cycling while maintaining triple-phase-boundary accessibility for O₂ and protons.
- Achieving high activity at ultralow Pt loading (0.04 mg cm⁻²) provides a commercially viable pathway to reduce cathode catalyst cost by >75% relative to current automotive targets.

## 2. Chlorine-Induced Dangling Nitrogen-Bridged Dual-Atom Iron Catalyst for Highly Efficient Oxygen Reduction

**Authors:** Zhang et al. (Fanchao Zhang)
**Journal:** *Journal of the American Chemical Society* 148(5), 5167–5178 (2026) · [DOI: 10.1021/jacs.5c17034](https://doi.org/10.1021/jacs.5c17034)

- Chlorine introduction into a di-iron nitrogen-carbon catalyst (Fe₂NC-Cl) creates an out-of-plane dangling N-bridge connecting the dual Fe sites, relieving steric hindrance around the active centers and optimising adsorption–desorption equilibrium of oxygen intermediates.
- The Fe₂NC-Cl catalyst achieves a half-wave potential of **E₁/₂ = 0.924 V** in 0.1 M HClO₄, which is among the highest reported for non-Pt catalysts in acidic electrolyte.
- Durability is outstanding: after 50,000 potential cycles the catalyst shows a potential decay of only **0.067 μV per cycle**, far below the DOE target tolerance.
- DFT calculations confirm that the Cl-induced geometric distortion shifts the d-band centre of Fe, reducing the O₂ adsorption over-binding that typically limits Fe–N–C sites.
- This Cl-bridging strategy represents a general design lever for dual-atom M–N–C catalysts in acidic ORR, with clear prospects for integration into PEMFC cathodes.

## 3. Lanthanum-Induced Quasi-Covalent Bonding and Chemical Potential Bridging in Pt₃Co Intermetallic Catalysts for Durable Fuel Cells

**Authors:** Li et al. (Chuanfu Li)
**Journal:** *Journal of the American Chemical Society* 148(3), 3174–3184 (2026) · [DOI: 10.1021/jacs.5c16692](https://doi.org/10.1021/jacs.5c16692)

- Lanthanum (La) is incorporated into the L1₂-Pt₃Co intermetallic framework, shifting metal–metal bonding from purely metallic character toward quasi-covalent Pt–La and Pt–Co interactions that resist segregation under electrochemical conditions.
- La doping reduces the core–shell chemical potential gradient, thereby suppressing Co migration from the particle interior to the surface where it is susceptible to oxidative dissolution.
- The La–Pt₃Co/C catalyst achieves a mass activity of **1.29 A mg⁻¹Pt** and a peak power density of **1.27 W cm⁻²** in H₂–O₂ PEMFC single-cell tests.
- Chemical potential bridging lowers the thermodynamic driving force for Pt leaching, providing a mechanistic rationale for simultaneous activity and stability enhancement.
- The La-doping strategy is transferable to other rare-earth-modified L1₂ intermetallics, widening the design space for durable, high-performance PEMFC cathode catalysts.

## 4. Weakening the Oxophilicity of PtGd Alloy via Nitrogen-Induced 5d/4f–2p Orbital Interaction for Efficient Fuel Cell Cathode Performance

**Authors:** Peng et al. (Zhuang group)
**Journal:** *Advanced Functional Materials* 36(24) (2026) · [DOI: 10.1002/adfm.202524485](https://doi.org/10.1002/adfm.202524485)

- Nitrogen doping into a PtGd alloy catalyst modifies Pt 5d/Gd 4f–N 2p orbital coupling, downshifting the Pt d-band centre and weakening *OH binding that otherwise limits ORR kinetics in Pt–rare-earth systems.
- The optimised PtGdN/C catalyst achieves a mass activity of **0.85 A mg⁻¹Pt** in 0.1 M HClO₄, more than twice that of the undoped PtGd/C benchmark.
- In H₂–O₂ fuel cell single-cell tests, PtGdN/C delivers a peak power density of **1.68 W cm⁻²** and a fuel-cell mass activity of **0.49 A mg⁻¹Pt**.
- After a **30,000-cycle** accelerated durability test, mass activity retention is **83.7%** (only 16.3% decay), confirming that N doping stabilises the Pt 5dz²–*OH 2pz hybridisation against dealloying.
- This work establishes nitrogen co-doping as a scalable strategy to concurrently address the over-binding problem of Pt–RE alloys and their known corrosion susceptibility.

## 5. Dynamic Vibration-Coupled Energy Transfer for Boosting ORR Catalysts in Fuel Cells

**Authors:** Liang et al. (Chenjia Liang)
**Journal:** *Journal of the American Chemical Society* 148(5), 5115–5124 (2026) · [DOI: 10.1021/jacs.5c15936](https://doi.org/10.1021/jacs.5c15936)

- Flexible tert-butylsulfonylcalix[4]arene (tBuC[4]A) is anchored on PtCo nanoparticles; upon O₂ adsorption the exothermic event excites tBuC[4]A vibrations (**0.126 eV per molecule**) that propagate via phonon–phonon scattering to adjacent Pt sites.
- Vibrational energy transfer resonantly weakens *OH surface binding, reducing the OH* desorption barrier by **51%** (from 0.375 eV to 0.183 eV), directly addressing the rate-limiting step in Pt-based ORR.
- Simulations indicate **13.2% energy utilisation efficiency** of the exothermic O₂ adsorption event, representing a previously unrecognised intra-catalyst energy recycling mechanism.
- The tBuC[4]A–PtCo catalyst shows significantly enhanced rotating-disk electrode activity and H₂–O₂ fuel-cell performance relative to bare PtCo, validating the phonon-mediated rate enhancement in a device setting.
- Vibration-coupled energy transfer (VCET) opens an entirely new design axis for ORR catalysts: engineering molecular adsorbate flexibility to redirect thermodynamic energy toward endothermic desorption steps.

## 6. Designing Co–N/C Cathode Catalysts with Dense Atomic Cobalt Sites for Enhanced PEMFC Performance

**Authors:** Gong et al.
**Journal:** *Advanced Science* 13(2), e2516060 (2026) · [DOI: 10.1002/advs.202516060](https://doi.org/10.1002/advs.202516060)

- A Co–N/C catalyst is synthesised with an exceptionally high cobalt loading of **3 wt%**, with all Co exclusively in atomically dispersed tetrapyridinic Co–N₄ coordination environments confirmed by EXAFS.
- Electrochemical quantification reveals an active site density of **3.58 × 10¹⁹ sites g⁻¹**, the high accessible site density being directly responsible for the catalyst's superior current output.
- ORR half-wave potential reaches **E₁/₂ = 0.76 V** at a practical low loading of **0.2 mg cm⁻²**, with minimal mass-transport losses.
- In H₂–O₂ PEMFC single-cell tests, the catalyst delivers a peak power density exceeding **1.3 W cm⁻²** (iR-corrected), competitive with state-of-the-art Fe–N–C benchmarks.
- This work shows that maximising atomic Co site density—rather than relying on metal clustering or Fe-based sites—is a viable route to fuel-cell-level PGM-free ORR performance.

## 7. Reconfiguring Ionomers in Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cell Catalyst Layer to Promote Multi-Species Transport and Durability

**Authors:** Yue et al.
**Journal:** *Angewandte Chemie International Edition* 65, e202516035 (2026) · [DOI: 10.1002/anie.202516035](https://doi.org/10.1002/anie.202516035)

- Porous graphene nanosheet@Nafion composite ionomers are introduced into the catalyst layer; the graphene nanosheets disrupt the lamellar Nafion configuration, converting flat ionic films into interconnected ionic microchannels that promote continuous proton conduction to catalyst sites.
- Proton transport in the reconfigured catalyst layer is enhanced **5.0-fold** relative to a conventional Nafion-only catalyst layer, reducing high-frequency resistance substantially.
- Oxygen diffusion to the triple-phase boundary increases **3.3-fold**, alleviating the primary mass-transport limitation at high current densities with reduced Pt loading.
- Rated power density (at 0.6 V) improves by **1.70×** and peak power density by **1.48×** versus the Nafion baseline MEA.
- The composite ionomer approach also improves water management and reduces membrane flooding at high current density, providing dual activity and durability benefits without altering the Pt catalyst.

## 8. Ionomer Distribution Control via Thiophene S-Modification of Carbon Support for High-Power Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells

**Authors:** et al.
**Journal:** *Nature Communications* 16, art. 66813 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1038/s41467-025-66813-6](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-66813-6)

- Thiophene-type sulfur (C-S-C) species are covalently introduced onto the carbon black support surface; the sulfonic group on Nafion's side chains is attracted to thiophene-S, drawing ionomer uniformly across the catalyst layer rather than allowing it to congregate around Pt nanoparticles.
- FIB/slice-STEM EDX mapping and molecular dynamics simulations confirm that thiophene-S modification produces a significantly more homogeneous ionomer spatial distribution compared to unmodified Ketjenblack.
- The strategy is validated at a low total Pt loading of **0.125 mg cm⁻²** (anode 0.025, cathode 0.1 mg cm⁻²) at **94 °C, 100% RH, 150 kPa backpressure** on a **25 cm²** active electrode area.
- Uniform ionomer distribution reduces local O₂ and proton transport resistance at triple-phase boundaries, recovering performance that is otherwise lost when Pt loading is decreased below the MEA performance cliff.
- This support-surface chemistry route to ionomer control is compatible with existing Pt/C catalysts, offering an immediately deployable strategy for reducing Pt cost without catalyst reformulation.

## 9. From Seeds to Cell: Improving PEMFC Performance and Durability by Seed-Mediation Synthesis for PtNiIr ORR Nanocatalysts

**Authors:** Pan et al. (Lujin Pan, Peter Strasser group — TU Berlin)
**Journal:** *Advanced Science* 12, e2505958 (2025) · [DOI: 10.1002/advs.202505958](https://doi.org/10.1002/advs.202505958)

- A seed-mediation synthesis protocol grows ternary **PtNiIr** alloy nanoparticles with controlled composition and size distribution; Ir incorporation into PtNi is used to stabilise the ternary framework against Ni dissolution in acidic PEMFC conditions.
- MEA testing across multiple carbon supports shows that seed-mediated PtNiIr on **porous carbon** outperforms the same catalyst on solid carbon in both peak power density and durability, attributed to nanoparticle deposition inside pore channels that physically impedes agglomeration.
- Non-seed-mediated reference PtNiIr catalysts show substantially faster performance decline during AST cycling, confirming that nucleation control directly determines long-term stability.
- Systematic variation of Pt loading and carbon support in the full MEA setting demonstrates that seed-mediation synthesis variables can be tuned independently of the catalyst layer formulation.
- This seed-mediation approach is demonstrated as a general, scalable method for ternary Pt-alloy ORR catalysts, with direct transfer to MEA-level performance validation providing a clear path toward automotive fuel cell cathode applications.
